Nocturnes

Chopin's Nocturnes are among his most beautiful and expressive works, inspired by the nocturnes of Irish composer John Field but elevated to a new level of emotional depth and pianistic sophistication.

Nocturnes, Op. 9

Year: 1830-1832

Dedication: Camille Pleyel

Contents:

  • No. 1 in B♭ minor
  • No. 2 in E♭ major
  • No. 3 in B major

Nocturnes, Op. 15

Year: 1830-1833

Dedication: Ferdinand Hiller

Contents:

  • No. 1 in F major
  • No. 2 in F♯ major
  • No. 3 in G minor

Nocturnes, Op. 27

Year: 1835

Dedication: Countess d'Appony

Contents:

  • No. 1 in C♯ minor
  • No. 2 in D♭ major

Nocturnes, Op. 32

Year: 1837

Dedication: Madame de Billing

Contents:

  • No. 1 in B major

Nocturnes, Op. 37

  • No. 1 in G minor
  • No. 2 in G major

Polonaises

Chopin's Polonaises reflect his deep connection to his Polish heritage. These works transformed a traditional dance form into powerful expressions of national pride and resilience.

Polonaises, Op. 26

Year: 1834-1835

Contents:

  • No. 1 in C♯ minor
  • No. 2 in E♭ minor

Polonaises, Op. 40

Year: 1838-1839

Contents:

  • No. 1 in A major "Military"
  • No. 2 in C minor

Polonaise in F♯ minor, Op. 44

Year: 1841

Polonaise in A♭ major, Op. 53 "Heroic"

Year: 1842

One of Chopin's most famous and technically demanding works.
